# Classification Rules

## Evidence channels

Keep these distinct:

1. caption/body;
2. hashtags;
3. explicit mentions;
4. account context;
5. visuals;
6. audio speech;
7. on-screen text;
8. parent/child relationship;
9. reviewed historical examples.

Record which channels were actually available.

## Relevant content

A tag alone is not always enough. Determine whether the product, discussion, creator context, visuals, speech or on-screen text genuinely relates to the brand.

Do not discard content because the caption lacks a brand mention when visual or audio evidence establishes relevance.

## Legacy American Apparel

Discontinued products can be relevant throwbacks:

- disco pants;
- skirts;
- leotards;
- bodysuits;
- legacy dresses;
- other historic fashion categories.

The caption does not need to say “throwback.” The visible item can establish legacy context.

## Needs Review

Route to review when:

- confidence is low;
- channels conflict;
- media is incomplete;
- carousel children are missing;
- video/audio are unavailable;
- product identification is uncertain;
- the tag is incidental;
- the source link is broken.

## Human feedback

Do not overwrite the machine result. Store human labels separately and append audit history.
